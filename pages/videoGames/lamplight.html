  Lamplight Group Presentation

This Narrative Game Design Document (GDD) was created for the Fall 2024 semester of Writing for Video
Games with Professor Telep. Taught with the tight deadlines of the video game industry in mind, students of this
class were required to complete both an individual and group GDD at the same time. This is my group GDD from
this class with a team we titled the Mystery Buggy Buddies (otherwise known as Team 4).

All of the team contributed about equally to the ideas behind the Lamplight, however, within the document itself,
I contributed the title, razor statement, focus paragram, and the world section of the text.

  Razor Statement: 

            Lamplight is a whimsical point-and-click adventure mystery game where the player plays
            as a moth detective named Merlock Moth who is trying to solve the murder of Timmy
            Termite, a demolition expert. Merlock explores the city to interrogate insect suspects,
            find clues, and exonerate himself

  Focus Paragraph: 

            Lamplight is a whimsical point-and-click adventure mystery game where the player plays
            as a moth detective named Merlock Moth who is trying to solve the mystery of Timmy
            Termite, a demolition expert who was mysteriously killed in Merlock’s office. Merlock
            explores Buzzburg, a bug-inhabited city inside of an abandoned trash can, in order to
            interrogate suspects including an ant construction worker, a queen bee barkeep, a ladybug
            idol, a centipede scientist, and a firefly firefighter. The player will traverse the three
            levels of Buzzburg—the Upper Metamorphosociety, the middle Carapace Corner, and the
            lower Mafia-controlled Spider-Square Slums—to try to solve the murder and clear
            Merlock’s name.
