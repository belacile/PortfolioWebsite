About Me

I'm Caleb Rosenfeld—a writer, coder, game developer, video editor, and amateur artist.

I have been taking college classes since I started dual enrolling at fifteen years old back in the fall semester of 2019.

However, even before this, I possessed a creative and academic mind, always striving to learn new things. I have attempted many creative outlets ranging from simple digital art to crafting entire Youtube videos. However, I discovered my love for storytelling at age fifteen when I wrote a character backstory for my first ever game of Dungeons and Dragons—or D&D for short. The game sparked my imagination, and inspired me to pursue storytelling as my hobby. To this day, I still play (and DM) as often as I'm able, and it has made me extremely confident in my skills as a collaborative storyteller. In D&D, every voice at the table contributes to the greater narrative, guiding the story in ways that no single person at the table could possibly conceive on our own.

Although my desire to tell stories of quests and monsters has remained indomitable, I still decided to pursue Computer Science in college, as this would teach me many skills that would help me satiate the other side of my brain—the side that loves math, puzzles, and abstract problem-solving. Interestingly enough, a good portion of my love for problem-solving comes from video game puzzles. Games that really scratched this itch for me from a young age were Portal 2, Monument Valley, and all of the LEGO video games.
