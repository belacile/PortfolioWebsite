About Me

Hello and welcome to my portfolio! Let me take your coat and hat before we sit down.
Would you like anything to drink? A glass of water? A soda?
Is that chair too firm? Here, I have a pillow to help out a bit.
Alright, now that we're seated, it's nice to meet you!

I'm Caleb Rosenfeld—a writer, coder, game developer, video editor, and amateur artist.

I have been taking college classes since I started dual enrolling at fifteen years old back in the fall semester of 2019. Now that I am twenty-one, I have been commited to writing and coding seriously for six and three years respectively.

However, even before this, I possessed a creative and academic mind, always striving to learn new things. I have
attempted many creative outlets ranging from simple digital art to crafting entire Youtube videos. However,
I discovered my love for storytelling at age fifteen when I wrote a character backstory for my first ever game of
Dungeons and Dragons—or D&D for short. The game sparked my imagination, and inspired me to pursue
storytelling as my hobby. To this day, I still play (and Dungeon Master) as often as I'm able, and it has made me
extremely confident in my skills as a collaborative storyteller. In D&D, every voice at the table contributes to the
greater narrative, guiding the story in ways that no single person at the table could possibly conceive on our own.

Although my desire to tell stories of quests and monsters has remained indomitable, I still decided to pursue
Computer Science in college, as this would teach me many skills that would help me satiate the other side of my
brain—the side that loves math, puzzles, and abstract problem-solving. Interestingly enough, a good portion of
my love of problem-solving comes from video game puzzles. Games that really scratched this itch for me from a
young age were Portal 2, Monument Valley, and all of the LEGO video games.
